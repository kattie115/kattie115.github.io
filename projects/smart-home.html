Smart Home AC

Description

Smart Home AC was a startup idea for a plug-in produch and mobile app that interfaces with your existing devices (air conditioner, heater, smart lamps...) from your phone to manage your home remotely, and allows you better control over them, all while saving you time, money and energy.

The Challenge

The ever-rising costs of energy and awareness of environmental issues have inspired many of us to live greener.

While a lot of extra costs result from poor insulation and outdated heating systems in old homes, around 16% of all electricity costs are from running the (around 60 to 70 percent of your electric bill in the summer months if you live in a slightly hotter climate) devices.

Now think about how much money you spend on heating and cooling? Just running your AC over the summer is gonna result in appx. 200â‚¬ annually.

And a lot of these costs are actually from using our devices inefficiently - forgetting to turn the AC off while we're away, constant unregulated temperatures. Which is a huge problem, and many holmes already use solutions as smart thermostats, which learn our behavior and reduce the unnecessary waste.The product is designed with the idea to help monitor and control your home without being at the expense of convenience or price.

The Solution

I started out by making a user flow, to come up with the main features of the application that would solve the real user's needs. It allowed me to explore the hierarchy and the full structure of the app.

User Flow

Because my main point was to make it as convenient as possible to use the app, I focused on simplifying the sign-up process - using the wifi to connect the devices.

If someone decided to pair an already connected device, the application sends a notification to the original owner to grant the permission to others to use and control the device.

Main screen, loading screen, dashboard

When viewing the specific device, it offers the option to turn it on/off, temperature adjustment with the information on current temp. and how long it will take for that to reach. Users can set the devices to operate on pre-set schedules.

Scheduling consists out of a timeline where the user can select specific dates and times for device with the drag interaction to set a block of time they found the be the most efficient to run their device.

When the user leaves their home wi-fi range, the app interprets this as them leaving their apartment. If they left the device on, the app sends them a notification to either remind them to turn it off to save on energy consumption.

Device screen, scheduling, notification

The power consumption and stats for temperature regulations are offered from the tabbed menu. Users can view specific time periods (daily, weekly, monthly) on how much energy the devices used or changes in temperature. It displays it with the bar and line graph, as well as a list.
